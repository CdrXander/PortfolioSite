Movie Shelf

What do I want to watch?

All good software starts with a problem. My problem is that I keep forgetting what movies I want to see, either in theaters or at home. Friends come over for a movie night, and we all ask, what should we watch?

I built MovieShelf as a solution to my problem. It is a front-end only application built with AngularJS. It uses browser LocalStorage to keep track of a user's collection.

CinemaNode

When LocalStorage is not enough...

I showed my MovieShelf project to a friend of mine who works in film. Her immediate thought was to expand it to become "GoodReads.com for Movies." As a fan of both movies, and GoodReads.com, I liked the idea.

MovieShelf had a few limitations that were big obstacles in the way of expanding it into GoodReads.com for movies. It didn't have multiple user support, didn't persist data beyond the current browser, and suffered from some data optimization issues. These were big enough issues, that I wanted to redesign the project from the ground up. As a new project, it needed a new name. CinemaNode.

The biggest addition to CinemaNode was the addition of a back end. Using Node.js and PosgreSQL I added support for multiple users and data persistence. This is a project that I'm pretty excited about, and continue to work on.

SmartTable

"Sometimes I don't want to deal with people..."

At DevMountain, when my classmates and I were discussing group projects, Sid Jacobs came up with the idea for an application that would enable restaurant patrons to get prompt, accurate service without having to wait for their waiter to pass, or in the case of introverts, without having to really deal with people at all.

I worked as part of a four-man team to build the SmartTable application. Beyond the basic challenges of writing code, we learned how to work as a team on the same codebase. Git, pull, push, merge and branch became part of our daily lexicon. I worked mostly on building the back end for the application, including building authorization and authentication from scratch and encrypting passwords, but I also built several components in React.
